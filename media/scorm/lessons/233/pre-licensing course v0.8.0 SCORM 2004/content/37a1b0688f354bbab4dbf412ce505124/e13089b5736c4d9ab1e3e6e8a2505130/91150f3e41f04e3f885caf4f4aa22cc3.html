What Happens When You Drink?

Alcohol is one of the fastest substances absorbed by the body


About 20 percent of the alcohol is absorbed in the stomach and about 80 percent is absorbed in the small intestine.

Before you can even feel the effect of the alcohol on your system, it is already in the bloodstream. You do not know when you have had too much to drink until it is too late. This is similar to sunburn, by the time you feel it, you are already burned


On an empty stomach, a shot of whiskey is in your arm in about 7 seconds! It touches every major part of your body within two minutes.


The liver can only process a 1/3 ounce of alcohol per hour. This is a fixed rate.

Only time - not black coffee or a cold shower - will sober up a person who is impaired by alcohol
